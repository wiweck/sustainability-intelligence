# Sustainability Intelligence standards

These rules govern every intelligence item before publication.

## Evidence before interpretation

Each record must distinguish:

1. Verified facts from analysis.
2. Interpretation from prediction.
3. Direct opportunities from emerging signals.
4. Individual Expert Fit from Nonprofit Fit.
5. Confirmed eligibility from possible consortium pathways.
6. Evidence strength from model confidence.
7. Recommended action from the user’s final decision.

## Source requirements

- Prioritise original notices and authoritative public sources.
- Treat paid aggregators only as discovery clues.
- Locate and verify the underlying public source wherever possible.
- Preserve the source URL, issuer, publication date, retrieval time and verification state.
- Do not publish fabricated, expired, duplicate, inaccessible or weakly sourced items as active intelligence.

## Analysis requirements

- Use type-specific eligibility, evidence, prediction and ranking rules.
- Explain relevance to India without limiting discovery to India.
- Assess stakeholder perspectives, counter-scenarios, downside risks and uncertainty.
- Keep Individual Expert Fit separate from Nonprofit Fit.
- Do not treat commercial tenders as automatically relevant nonprofit opportunities.
- Consider credible consortium pathways where an organisation can contribute a substantial intervention component.

## Editorial boundary

Automated analysis cannot publish an item by itself. A final editorial review must confirm authenticity, deduplication, classification, evidence, fit, risk and uncertainty.
